About Adam

Hello! My name is Adam Gencarelli, and I'm a Software Engineering student currently studying at Western University. I am in my fifth and final year of study after recently completing a 16-month internship as a Software Developer at IBM Canada. Starting May 2017, I will be actively seeking full time employment as a Software Engineer.

I consider myself to be a very dedicated individual. As an intern at IBM, I was thrust into a difficult product (IBM Business Process Manager) with little time to learn my role and begin contributing. I was able to quickly adapt to my new environment, proving to my team that I could be an effective developer for them. I was able to grow with my team, starting in a software testing role and moving up to a junior developer. I developed a plethera of valuable skills that I will now look to transfer into new and exciting roles as a full time software engineer.

I began coding in my first year of engineering by learning C++. I immediately developed a love for programming, and I constantly chose to be working on fun programming tasks rather than my school assignments. The one thing I love the most about programming is that though it is a career, it feels like I get to play a game for my job. Solving difficult problems is more like a challenging puzzle to me, and implementing a new website is exciting to create and design. Because of this, I am motivated to continue to improve my skills and discover new quirks about what I do.

Outside of work, I enjoy dabbling in several different sports, such as volleyball, hockey, soccer, ultimate frisbee, or football. I enjoy getting together with friends to watch big sports games, or the latest episode of Game of Thrones. I also enjoy spending time teaching others about different technologies. I have volunteered with two organizations to provide guidance and tutorials on new web frameworks, which I find very rewarding.

My biggest aspirations in life are to graduate as a software engineer, find a job working on a product that I love with the ability to see my code in action, and to get a puppy.
